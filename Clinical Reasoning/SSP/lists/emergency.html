4.4. Emergency symptoms findings and situations

  • acute abdominal, epigastric pain
  • acute chest; epigastric, arm, jaw, tooth pain
  • acute neurological deficits
  • acute severe dyspnoea
  • acute severe headache, meningism
  • burn
  • colic
  • haematemesis
  • intoxication, poisoning
  • mydriasis, myosis
  • self harm and suicide attempt
  • severe hypertension, severe hypotension
  • severe acute blood loss
  • sudden changes of mental status such as confusion, delusion, (auto-)aggressive behaviour
  • syncope, loss of consciousness
  • cardiorespiratory disturbances and arrest
  • seizures
  • uncomplicated common trauma
